A Carbon Footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (including carbon dioxide and methane) that are generated by our actions. The average carbon footprint for a person in the United States is 16 tons, one of the highest rates in the world. Globally, the average carbon footprint is closer to 4 tons. Understanding your carbon footprint is like looking in the mirror and realizing your impact on the world. It's about recognizing how every action, from turning on a light to driving a car, contributes to the greenhouse gases that warm our planet. By calculating your carbon footprint, you gain clarity on your environmental footprint, enabling you to make more mindful choices. It's not just about reducing emissions; it's about taking responsibility for our planet's health and ensuring a brighter future for generations to come. Food / Daily Meals

The emission factor for food is 1.25 kgCO2/meal.

Food production contributes significantly to carbon emissions, accounting for about 31% of global greenhouse gas emissions, largely from livestock, deforestation, and fertilizer use (FAO, 2021). Meat and dairy, especially from ruminant animals, have the highest emissions per calorie produced. Shifting to plant-based diets and reducing food waste can greatly reduce the food sector's carbon footprint (IPCC, 2022).
